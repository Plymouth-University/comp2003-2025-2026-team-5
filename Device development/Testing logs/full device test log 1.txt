The test lasted approximately 12 minutes. The device is set to initiate a send cycle every 10 seconds, however
the time for the cycle to complete alters the interval between cycles. I will be uploading screenshots of the
test shortly.

During the test i accessed the web server with my phone and observed the accuracy of the GPS coordinates. At times
it would deviate from my exact position. This is likely due to the exact path I took, which included the path running
alongside the western wall of the Charles Seale-Hayne library and the eastern edge of the campus park. It is likely
that the concrete in the walls of the library reflected the signals being received by the device and/or the GPS
satellites and introduced a degree of inaccuracy.

Despite this, the least accurate result produced during the test was a 20.5 metre inaccuracy whilst walking alongside
the library. In a real life emergency scenario, 20.5 metres is an extremely precise result, and falls well within line
of sight. In the event a user strays into a difficult environment such as a forest, this would not be the case, however
a device such as the one we are creating is unlikely to function correctly in such an environment anyway without a 
clear view of the sky.
